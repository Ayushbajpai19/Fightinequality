    National Media Festival

    *THE FIRST TIME EVER MEDIA FESTIVAL TO CREATE CHANGE MAKERS IN THE FIELD OF MEDIA AND MASS COMMUNICATION* We warmly extend an invitation for you to attend and cover our 3 day media festival  ‘NATIONAL MEDIA FESTIVAL – NMF, 2021’ happening on Dec 3rd to 5th at Buddha Research Institute, Lucknow. First of its kind in India, the media festival is being organised by The Culture Factory Lucknow in association with Fight Inequality Alliance – FIA India. The 3 day festival will host experts from digital, print and electronic media; theatre, film and literature and some known social faces. The audience will be a combination of mass comm. students, aspiring media professionals and civil society members of Lucknow. Each day will have panel discussions (90mins each), workshops and interactive sessions from 10:00am to 5:30pm. Overall 15 panel discussions on media and related areas will be conducted to enlighten the audience. Our idea is to create change makers and informed individuals in this field and take journalism to next level of credibility in the ongoing scenario. And we hope that this event will provide an opportunity for you to garner youth perspectives by connecting with the students.

    बराबरी के नारों के साथ समाप्त हुआ 16 दिन का एक्टिविज़्म समारोह

    National Media Festival concludes with the pledge to become a responsible and aware media/journalist** The 3-day National Media Festival concluded today at Buddha Auditorium Gomtinagar. This festival was organized for the students of media and mass comm and it was attended by the experts of media, academics, theatre, art and literature from all over the country.* In this 3-day festival, Opportunities and challenges of media world , role of media, understanding role of language, news sense, is media patriarchal and gender biased? , fake news, what is news and many such important topics were discussed. Today's session started with video editing workshop. * Arif Jafar - Naaz Foundation, Darvesh - Social Activist and Naseeruddin Senior Journalist and Researcher took second session on role of media in covering disabled section, lgbt and transgenders and excluded communities. Understanding their issues and challenges is much needed to represent them appropriately. The third session was on * Fake News * by *Dr. Avinash Kumar from Delhi and Naseeruddin ji, who presented their perspectives and ongoing scenario around us. The program launched the song 'Bano Nayi Soch' which is made to support  the cause of daughters and their empowerment.* In the last session Vinod Mall, former DGP Gujarat and Rajeev Dhyani, social activist and satirist shared thoughts with the children and encouraged them. *All the students RECITED the Preamble together and took an oath that they would play the role of a responsible media person and citizen. Upasana who was the organizer of the program issued this press release. Sanhita, Aman, Himanshu, Adarsh, Pushpendra, Shruti, Abhishek and Kavya and other young members were behind the successful organization of the program.

    16 Days of Activism" campaign begins in various colleges from International violence against Women protest day to International Human Rights Day.* International day of Protest on violence against Women,25 November and The "International 16 Days of Activism" campaign has been started which will end on 10 December International Human Rights Day. It is being organized by Oxfam India and FIA (Samata Network). The campaign was inaugurated on 25 November at College of Fine Arts, Lucknow University. On this occasion a beautiful poetry exhibition based on gender discrimination and marriage without consent was organized which was inaugurated yesterday by HOD, Fine Arts Department "Sanjeev Gautam" and today it was done by "Shikha Pandey ji", HOD of Fine Arts Department of Goyal Institute. Whole world celebrates this 16-day campaign and different dates have their own significance, and all of them in some way or the other connect with the issue of discrimination/exploitation. Here are the details- 25 November - International Day for the Elimination of Violence Against Women 29 November - International Women's Human Rights Day 1 December - World AIDS Day 5 December - International Volunteer Day for Economic and Social Development December 6 – Anniversary of the Montreal Massacre, which is observed in Canada as the National Day of Remembrance and Action on Violence Against Women 10 December - International Human Rights Day and the anniversary of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. This exhibition will be organized daily from November 25 to December 10 in 15 colleges of Lucknow such as Goyal Institute, Karamat Girls, Avadh Girls, Navyug Girls etc. Its objective is to raise awareness among the youth about gender stereotypes, domestic violence against women, laws on raising the age of marriage, gender bias and discrimination in the society. At the same time, an understanding has to be made against marriage without consent. Sanhita Mishra, the main organizer of the program, had a discussion and question-answer session on these issues with the youth. All the students of Arts College and Goyal Institute enthusiastically participated in the exhibition, expressed their views and were encouraged. The exhibition was also seen by the teachers and greatly appreciated. Most of the youth were curious about the information about International Violence against women Protest Day. They said that this topic and its awareness is necessary. There was an ideological exchange with the youth on many deep issues like violence against women, art, culture, air social inequality etc. Various types of mind games and creative activities like poster making, poetry writing, and slogan writing were also organized under the leadership of youth activists Suhani, Ishika and their colleagues, to further strengthen the ideological understanding of the youth

    If music be the food of love, play on! A musical evening was organised on Saturday in Sheroes Cafe at Gomtinagar by Indian People’s Theatre Association, Dastak Manch and Samta Network to break the gloomy monotony of pandemic and have a small get together to celebrate the spirit of humanity. The evening had music lovers and social activists from all round the city. Surbhi Modi, Anil Sharma, Saman Habib, Amit Mishra, Rashmi, Rakesh, Meena Rana, Shubhra, Smriti Singh, Rafat, Rajeev Dhyani and many others were seen enjoying the evening. The event also announced winners of the quiz organised “Bhagat Singh Se Dosti '' which was organised in March on Bhagat Singh's birth anniversary. The quiz questioned students on the life and work of legendary revolutionary Bhagat Singh. Anjali Pandey, Priyanshi Bajpai and Anukrati Srivastava bagged the top three honours respectively, while Gaurav Pal came fourth and Himesh Kashyap and Gaurav Kumar shared the fifth prize. Ayushi, Anukrati and Piyush who came first, second and third respectively in the one minute video making contest - Instagram Reels, on the topic “Aazadi Ka Haasil” based on India’s independence were also felicitated. Saman Habib, Scientist CDRI, Rajeev dhyani, Satirist and political activist, Rakesh, General Secretary IPTA felicititated the winners. Vedika Srivastava and Safina Khan, Lucknow based artists sung melodies with social messages and the audience applauded. Rahul Srivastava and Deepshikha Srivastava from Pune-based musical band performed on some famous songs on Kabir and left everyone spellbound. Anil Sharma, additional chief Secretary Lucknow gave mementos to them and said such event should be held more often to promote brotherhood and equality. Tea, refreshments and cordial discussions with some funny and candid moments made the evening more memorable. The program also had audiences from colleges and universities of the city. Sanhita, Adarsh, Sangita, Veena, Shruti, Shuvalika and Upasana were the hosts of the event.
